Zhe Jiang 江哲

I am a postdoctoral scholar in the Computer Architecture Group, at the Department of Computer Science and Technology , University of Cambridge, United Kingdom.

I am broadly interested in the architecture, micro-architecture, and design automation for emerging computing systems, with a particular focus on improving safety, security, reliability, and timing-predictability of automotive, cloud and embedded systems.

Short Bio

  • I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Computer Architecture Group, at the Department of Computer Science and Technology, University of Cambridge, United Kingdom, where I closely work with Timothy M. Jones and Sam Ainsworth to explore microarchitectural supports to improve the security of out-of-ordering superscalar processors.

    • Formally, I worked as a

  • design engineer (2020-2022) at the Department of Central Engineering, ARM, United Kingdom, where I worked on the architecture and micro-architecture for server System-on-Chips (SoC)s. Our team have successfully delivered serval many-core (128/256-core) SoCs featured with Perseus and Zeus (ARM V9) processors, e.g., Genesis (3nm) and Bravo (5nm), for Microsoft's and BytaDance's data centres.

  • safety architect (2018 - 2020) at the Functional Safety Competency Centre (FSCC), Renesas Electronics, United Kingdom, where I work on the architecture and safety architecture for automotive micro-controllers. Our team have successfully delivered serval ASIL-D micro-controllers for Bosch's and Denso's ECUs, including RH850/U216 (28nm) -- the World's First 28nm Cross-Domain Flash MCU with virtualisation.

  • PhD. student (2014 - 2018) in the Real-Time Systems Group, at the Department of Computer Science, University of York, United Kingdom, where I supervised by Neil Audsley to explore the architecture and micro-architecture for real-time Network-on-Chips and Inputs/Outputs (I/Os). PhD. Thesis: Real-Time I/O System for Many-core Embedded Systems.
